Frequency-Domain Analysis of Thermoacoustic Coupling in LREs

IEEE Aerospace Conference 2026.

Download the full report (PDF)
System
Liquid rocket engine
Method
Frequency-domain analysis
Goal
Suppress pressure oscillations
Stage
Ignition transient

Combustion instability

In a liquid propellant rocket engine, heat release and chamber acoustics can lock into a feedback loop. The pressure oscillations that result, thermoacoustic instability, are one of the classic ways an engine tears itself apart, and they are most dangerous during ignition.

I developed a high fidelity numerical model of the engine and used frequency domain analysis to find the chamber geometry that best suppresses these oscillations, implementing a spatial noise propagation mitigation strategy for the ignition phase.

The animation at the top of the page shows the total sound pressure level field building through the chamber and nozzle. This work began during my internship at DHIO and grew into the conference paper.
