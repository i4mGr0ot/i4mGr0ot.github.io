What it does

SkyNET is an airfoil optimization framework built for the high lift configurations that unmanned aircraft need at low speed. Instead of guessing shapes and testing them, it works backwards: inverse design generates aerodynamic profiles from the lift and drag coefficients you actually want, then computational solvers automate the synthesis of airfoils for blown wing and high aspect ratio flight.

The sweep above is a parametric study from the framework, comparing high lift configurations against deflection and gap settings to find the best performing geometry.

From profile to deployable flap

A high lift airfoil is only useful if it can be built and deployed, so I also worked the mechanism side, designing slotted fowler flap deployment paths and Coanda surfaces and verifying their kinematics through the full range of motion.
