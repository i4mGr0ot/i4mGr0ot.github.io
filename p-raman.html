Czerny-Turner monochromator

Reading molecules with light

Raman spectroscopy identifies materials by the way they scatter laser light, without touching or destroying the sample. I architected a high precision Raman spectrophotometer around a Czerny Turner monochromator and laser optics for non destructive spectral analysis.

Chasing signal

The Raman signal is famously weak, so the engineering is all about throughput and noise. I optimised the optical throughput and the signal to noise ratio for detecting organic biosignatures in complex material matrices, integrating precision diffraction gratings and a CCD sensor to get high resolution spectra for geochemical and biological sampling.
