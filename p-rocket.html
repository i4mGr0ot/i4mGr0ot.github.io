Inside the chamber

At DHIO I ran high fidelity, mesh free CFD of the complex flow inside liquid propellant rocket engines. The focus was thermoacoustic instabilities and combustion dynamics, using frequency domain analysis to predict and mitigate the pressure oscillations that threaten an engine.

The animation at the top is the total sound pressure level field developing through the chamber and nozzle. I looked closely at the acoustic coupling inside the combustion chamber, with the goal of improving structural damping and ignition stability.

This internship is also where my IEEE Aerospace Conference research on thermoacoustic coupling started, so the project and the paper share a lineage.
