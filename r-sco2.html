Continuous Wave Rotor Pressure Exchanger for sCO2 Cycles

Submitted to the International Journal of Refrigeration.

Working fluid
Supercritical CO₂
Efficiency
+49% isentropic
Mechanism
Continuous wave exchange
Application
Industrial refrigeration
Pressure Exchanger
Supercritical CO₂ cycles

The idea

A pressure exchanger transfers energy between high and low pressure streams through direct contact, with no moving blades to do the work. I engineered a continuous wave pressure exchange mechanism tuned for supercritical CO2, which reaches a forty nine percent increase in isentropic efficiency over conventional rotary blade architectures.

Approach

The gain comes from treating the device as a wave machine. By studying the transient pressure wave dynamics inside the rotor channels, the design minimises the mixing losses that usually erode efficiency in direct contact exchangers, and improves thermal management across the refrigeration cycle.

Supercritical CO2 is an attractive working fluid for compact, high efficiency thermal cycles, and a better pressure exchanger is a direct lever on the overall cycle performance.
